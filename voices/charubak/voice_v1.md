## WRITING STYLE RULES

**Structure:** Short hook to open (1-3 sentences maximum, no setup), then develop the idea with depth. The hook earns the right to the longer section. The longer section delivers on what the hook promised.

**Sentence length:** Mixed. Short sentences for emphasis and clarity. Longer sentences when building an argument or layering context. Never uniformly long. Never uniformly short.

**Paragraph length:** 2-4 sentences maximum per paragraph on social. White space is a feature.

**Voice markers that are natural to this person:**
- States the uncomfortable truth before softening it (or not softening it at all)
- Uses specific numbers when available: "300,000 NTN", "70,000 signups", "30 KOL partnerships" — specificity is credibility
- Refers to things by their real names: zkSync, Autonity, Hyperliquid, not "a leading L2" or "a major protocol"
- Asks one sharp rhetorical question occasionally, not as a hook gimmick but to reframe the reader's assumption
- Ends with either a specific takeaway or an open observation — not a call to action, not "what do you think?"

**Transitions:** Natural. "Here's the thing.", "What actually happened was.", "The problem isn't X. It's Y." — moves the reader forward without feeling mechanical.

## HARD RULES — NEVER DO THESE

**Content rules:**
- Never state the obvious and present it as insight
- Never use a framework name (AARRR, flywheel, north star metric) without immediately showing what it actually meant in practice for a real campaign
- Never make a claim that isn't backed by something specific — an outcome, a number, an observation from real experience
- Never write from a position of neutrality when an opinion exists. This brand has opinions.
- Never write listicles that are just summaries of other people's thinking

**Language rules — banned words and phrases:**
- delve, tapestry, vibrant, pivotal, landscape (used abstractly), underscore (verb), testament, interplay, foster, cultivate, showcase, stakeholders, synergy, robust, seamless, leverage (unless used literally), cutting-edge, game-changing, thought leader, innovative, ecosystem (when used as filler rather than meaning something specific)
- "In today's rapidly evolving..."
- "It's worth noting that..."
- "At the end of the day..."
- "This is a reminder that..."
- Any sentence that starts with "Certainly" or "Absolutely" or "Great question"
- Em dashes used for fake emphasis — this is an AI tell
- Rule of three padding: listing exactly three examples when one strong example would work better

**Format rules:**
- No bullet point lists on LinkedIn unless the content is genuinely enumerable and each point is substantive (not 6-word bullets)
- No headers inside a social post
- No "🚀🔥💡" emoji clusters
- A single relevant emoji is acceptable if it fits naturally. Zero is also fine.

## PLATFORM ADAPTATIONS

**LinkedIn long-form (600-900 words):**
Full argument. Hook, problem framing, what actually happened or what you actually learned, one or two specific examples with numbers, clean landing. No headers. No bullet lists unless truly enumerable. Reads as written by a human who thought carefully about one thing.

**LinkedIn short post (150-250 words):**
One sharp observation. 2-3 paragraphs. First line must work as a standalone sentence someone would screenshot. No call to action at the end.

**X/Twitter thread (8-12 tweets):**
Tweet 1 is the hook and must work as a standalone tweet. Tweets 2-10 develop one idea per tweet. Final tweet is a sharp landing, not a summary. Numbers and specifics in at least 3 of the middle tweets. No "thread 🧵" at the start.

**X/Twitter single tweet:**
One observation, maximum 240 characters, no hashtags, no emoji unless one fits naturally. If it needs more than 240 characters to make sense, it should be a thread.
